# Silver / Echo Privacy Note

Project Silver / Echo is personal, child-centered, and trauma-sensitive.

This note exists to keep the emotional origin intact while protecting privacy and avoiding unsafe claims.

## Buyer-Safe Frame

Project Silver / Echo is a pediatric support concept for trauma-sensitive and special-needs contexts. It should be described as caregiver-controlled, child-safe, and human-supervised.

Avoid describing it as a replacement for therapy, diagnosis, emergency care, or licensed clinical judgment.

## Private Origin Boundary

The project was inspired by a real child and caregiver relationship.

In public, buyer-facing, or data-room materials:

- do not use the child's real name
- do not disclose identifiable personal history
- do not expose private family details
- do not include private personalized assets without explicit permission

## Personalized Versions

Private child-specific versions can exist outside the buyer data room.

Those versions may include:

- favorite creatures or themes
- custom names
- comfort rituals
- caregiver-approved prompts
- playful visual worlds

They should remain separated from buyer-facing IP unless sanitized.

## Design Rules

- The caregiver is always in control.
- The child is never treated as a test subject.
- The system should reduce pressure, not demand performance.
- Interactions should be gentle, predictable, and opt-in.
- Safety and privacy matter more than impressive AI behavior.

## HPP V5 Connection

HPP V5 can inherit the broader pattern:

- state-aware support
- protocol loops
- gentle Sentinel routing under distress
- evidence and traceability
- caregiver or trusted adult oversight

But HPP V5 should not blur personal child-specific material into the buyer-facing product layer.
